Jonathan A. Seward

I am a post-doctoral researcher in the Economics Department at Baylor University where my research is focused on health care markets, particularly provider responses to policy shocks, and mental health care in criminal justice. Although, these lines of research are distinct, both promise to inform policy: one stream focuses on anticompetitive effects of hospital consolidation and vertical integration, and the other stream focuses on policies to reduce suicidality and improve mental health of disadvantaged populations.

Principles of Microeconomics

Baylor University | Fall 2022

Principles of Microeconomics is an introductory undergraduate course that teaches the fundamentals of microeconomics. This course is designed to lay the foundation of economic behavior and markets to give you a different lens with which to view the world. In other words, at the end of the semester, you should begin to see your individual choices from a new perspective and why other people make their choices. We will explore how our individual choices and preferences affect which products are offered, how they are produced, and how they are allocated. This course should enable you to understand the foundations of microeconomic theory, solve basic microeconomic problems, and use these techniques to think about relevant policy questions in the real world.

Principles of Macroeconomics

Baylor University | Spring 2023

Principles of Macroeconomics is an introductory undergraduate course that teaches the fundamentals of macroeconomics. Principles of Macroeconomics will introduce you to the major issues of economic growth, business cycles, unemployment, and inflation that form the core of macroeconomics. Building on the basis principles of economics, we will develop a framework for understanding the role of monetary and fiscal policies and how they affect the economy. I hope that it will serve as a solid foundation upon which you build your understanding in economics, business, or similar disciplines.
